Ace Up

Overview:

AceUP is a tabletop first-person shooter (FPS) based on the classic card game of Blackjack, but with a unique twist: everyone is cheating. This game shifts the focus from relying on luck to that requiring skill and strategy, this offers a fresh take on the traditional Blackjack experience. Players must cheat to attempt to get a perfect '21'. However, if they get caught, they face the consequence of a starting a free-for-all brawl with all the other opponents.

Develop:Brighton:

AceUp was showcased at Develop:Brighton, where I presented the game in collaboration with the University of Sussex alongside other indie and larger studios. During the showcase, I gathered feedback from playtesters who helped identify bugs and glitches, this feedback aided in the game being published in early December of 2024.

Blackjack Logic:

The Blackjack logic in AceUP was developed using object-oriented programming to create a deck and card management system. A card management script handled shuffling, drawing, and resetting the deck for each round, while a player hand script calculated each player's hand value according to Blackjack rules, including Ace values and the ability to swap cards when cheating. Players could manipulate their hands, which required real-time cheat detection through custom scripts that flagged suspicious actions. If cheating was detected, the game seamlessly transitioned to an FPS-style combat sequence, where throwing cards served as ammunition.

Enemy Pathfinding

Enemy pathfinding was implemented using Unity’s NavMesh system, allowing AI-controlled enemies to navigate the environment effectively. By baking a NavMesh into the scene, enemies could select random positions based on their proximity to other AIs and the playable character. The pathfinding script, 'fightAI', determined whether an enemy should move towards the playable character, the closest player, or a random location, adapting to the gameplay.

Finding Home

Overview:

Finding Home is a story-driven game where you play as Remi, a young fox who has gotten lost and must find his way back to his parents. In this cozy narrative adventure, you interact with various residents in this small world, each offering unique dialogues and assistance. To help Remi navigate the area and find his parents, you must leave breadcrumbs along the way, creating a path to follow as you explore the island and uncover the stories within.

The game features 13 unique NPCs that players can interact with, each contributing to the story and world-building. Leave Something Behind:

Breadcrumbs were used to fit the theme of the game jam: 'Leave Something Behind'. Using Unity's Collider component and the OnTriggerExit() function, I was able to detect when and where breadcrumbs should be left behind. The use of breadcrumbs incentivizes players to communicate with the resident NPCs, as each one offers a varying amount to aid in the player's journey.

Player Movement:

As the lead programmer on this project, I took full responsibility for developing the code such as the movement mechanics, which was the core feature of Rebellion. I designed and implemented the dynamic parkour abilities, ensuring that Eve’s actions were responsive, and intuitive. The system I created enabled smooth transitions based on player input and velocity, resulting in a seamless and immersive experience. Through my work, I laid the foundation for the game’s movement system, making it integral to the gameplay and essential to the overall experience.

Click the image to view more!

Animation:

Eve features a dynamic animation system powered by a complex animation tree, which facilitates seamless transitions between a wide range of movements, such as climbing, vaulting over obstacles, and sliding under low fences. Each action is triggered by player input (e.g., pressing space to jump, shift to crouch), with Eve's movement influenced by her current velocity (for instance, shifting mid-run triggering a slide).
The animation system also adapts to various environments, activating unique animations when interacting with specific objects, like climbing a rope ladder or gradually slowing down when crouched. This depth of animation significantly enhances the immersive gameplay experience, with Eve’s model reacting realistically to changes in speed and momentum, making her movements feel natural and synchronized with the world around her.
